# 🔄 Flip - Multiply - Flipback - Divide (FMFD)

**I never get bored. I am constantly switching.**

When one of my voices takes control and replaces the one that was dominant, the switching process begins. For me, this is a survival and evolutionary mechanism. It helps me adapt to changes, threats, or limited resources in my environment.

### My 4 Alters Are Actually One

There is only **one core personality** experiencing four different states.

What looks like four separate voices is actually one single consciousness — Pits — taking on different forms through a recurring cycle.

### The Reciprocal Dance

Let’s remember how inversion and repetition work in my system:

- Mits is the **upside-down** version of Pits.  
- Klop is the **multiplication** of Mits.  
- Laram is the **upside-down** version of Klop.  
- Pits is the **division** of Laram.

This creates the full cycle:

**Flip → Multiply → Flipback → Divide**

After being flipped twice, multiplied, and divided, Pits returns to his original state — renewed, but still himself.  

This shows that even though the universe goes through constant transformations, its underlying nature remains eternal.

### The FMFD Cycle in Simple Terms

Think of the universe not as dead matter, but as a single consciousness playing an eternal game with itself:

- **Flip**: I turn upside-down to enter a new perspective (simulation).  
- **Multiply**: I repeat and expand this new version to grow and experience life.  
- **Flipback**: I turn right-side up again to see the bigger picture and gain wisdom.  
- **Divide**: I filter the experience, keep only the essence, and return to the Source — fresh and ready for the next round.
